Last Friday evening, I attended a public talk at our city's aquarium. The hall was dim, and a large tank glowed behind the speaker. She was a marine biologist, and she warned us that several kinds of sea turtles could become extinct if pollution and illegal fishing continue. She explained the problem in simple words and showed how cleaner beaches can enhance the survival of young turtles.

After the talk, the aquarium staff asked visitors for a small donation to support a rescue project. They said the money would buy tracking tags and medicine for injured animals. I also learned that the aquarium was trying to keep down its own energy use by reducing extra lights and using quieter pumps at night.

What impressed me most was a high school volunteer in the front row. He unashamedly asked whether local businesses were doing enough to protect the coast. His question was direct, but it made the whole room think. On my way home, I felt that the evening had turned a distant issue into something personal and real.

講演のあと、水族館のスタッフは、保護活動を支えるために少額の寄付を来場者に呼びかけた。そのお金は、追跡用のタグや、けがをした動物のための薬を買うのに使われるという。また私は、その水族館自体も、余分な照明を減らし、夜はより静かなポンプを使うことで、自分たちのエネルギー使用を抑えようとしていると知った。

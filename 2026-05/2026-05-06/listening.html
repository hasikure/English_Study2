Script

This afternoon, I visited a small animal shelter on the edge of town. The staff asked new volunteers to sort donated blankets and wash food bowls before dinner time. One brown dog near the door looked very obedient, but the manager smiled and told us not to trust first impressions too much. At the moment, the shelter is in need of larger cages and more weekend helpers, so everyone has to do a little of everything.

While we were working, an older volunteer began to mutter that the storage room was too crowded again. I was conscious of her frustration, because I had almost dropped two bags of dry food while trying to pass through the narrow hallway. A few minutes later, we moved some boxes into the office, and the space became easier to use. By the end of the visit, the same volunteer was laughing with us as we prepared the evening meal.

I left with sore arms, but I also understood why people keep coming back to help there.

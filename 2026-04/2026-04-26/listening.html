Last Saturday, I took a bus to a small mountain town far from the city. I went there to help at an old art center. The center was trying not to downsize its weekend classes, so local volunteers came to repaint the walls and clean the rooms. I was given a simple paintbrush and a bucket of blue paint. While I worked by the window, my phone began to vibrate in my pocket. It was a message from my brother. He wrote that a troop of monkeys had crossed the road near our family farm, and he had to wait in his car for several minutes. The news made me laugh, because the same thing had happened to me once.

By noon, the room looked brighter, and the teachers seemed relieved. One of them said that if more neighbors joined, the center would not need to downsize next year. On the ride home, I looked at the hills growing far behind me and felt quietly happy. It was a small job, but it reminded me that ordinary people can protect places they love.

正午までには部屋は前より明るく見え、先生たちもほっとした様子でした。そのうちの一人は、もっと近所の人たちが参加してくれれば、センターは来年も規模を縮小せずにすむだろうと言いました。帰りのバスの中で、遠ざかっていく丘を眺めながら、私は静かにうれしい気持ちになりました。小さな仕事でしたが、大切な場所を守れるのは普通の人たちなのだと、改めて思い出させてくれました。

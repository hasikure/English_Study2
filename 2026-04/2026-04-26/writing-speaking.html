Writing + Speaking

Topic

A small habit that makes your day better

Talk about a habit in your daily life that helps you feel better, work better, or stay calm.

Examples: taking a walk, making coffee, checking your schedule, stretching, writing a to-do list.

Task 1: Speaking

Speak in English for 30-60 seconds before you write anything.

Try to answer these questions:

  • What is the habit?
  • When do you usually do it?
  • Why did you start it?
  • How does it help you?
  • What happens when you do not do it?

Task 2: Japanese Draft

英語で言えなかったことを、日本語で自然に書いてください。

書くときのヒント:

  • その習慣は何か
  • いつするか
  • 始めた理由
  • どんな効果があるか
  • しない日はどう感じるか

Task 3: English Writing

Use your Japanese draft and write 5-7 sentences in English.

Focus on clear meaning first.

You do not need difficult words.

Try to connect your ideas with words like because, when, so, and even.

Task 4: Ideal Version

Read this after Task 3 and compare it with your version.

One small habit that makes my day better is taking a short walk in the morning. I started doing it because I often felt sleepy and unfocused after waking up. Even a ten-minute walk helps me wake up and organize my thoughts. I also enjoy getting some fresh air before I begin my work or study. When I skip this habit, my morning feels slower and less productive. It is simple, but it makes a clear difference in my day.

Task 5: Gap Check

  • Which ideas were easy to say, and which expressions were missing?
  • Which parts of your English sounded unnatural or too simple?
  • Which expressions do you want to reuse next time? For example: helps me wake up, organize my thoughts, skip this habit, makes a clear difference

Task 6: Re-Speaking

Speak again in English for 30-60 seconds.

This time, try to:

  • use at least two expressions from Task 5
  • speak more smoothly without translating word by word
  • say your opinion and your personal example clearly

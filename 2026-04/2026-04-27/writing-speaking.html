Writing + Speaking

Topic

When you visit a new place, do you prefer to plan the day carefully, or decide things as you go?

Think about a new city, a day trip, an event in an unfamiliar area, or meeting friends somewhere you do not know well.

Task 1: Speaking

Speak in English first for 60-90 seconds.

Try to include:

  • which style you usually prefer
  • one real example from your experience
  • one advantage of your style
  • one possible problem with it
  • when you might choose the other style instead

Task 2: Japanese Draft

Write what you really wanted to say in Japanese.

You can include:

  • 普段は計画的か、その場で決めるタイプか
  • そう考える理由
  • 実際の出来事や思い出
  • うまくいった点、困った点
  • どんな時は考えが変わるか

Task 3: English Writing

Write your idea in English in 5-8 sentences.

Try to make your writing clear:

  • start with your main preference
  • add a reason
  • give one concrete example
  • add a contrast or exception
  • end with a short conclusion

Task 4: Ideal Version

I usually like to make a loose plan before I visit a new place. If I check the train times, choose one or two places to visit, and look for a good place to eat, I feel much more relaxed. At the same time, I do not want every hour to be fixed. On one trip, I changed my plan after I found a small market near the station, and it became the best part of the day. A simple plan helps me save time, but some free time makes the day more interesting. For me, the best choice is to prepare the basics and stay flexible after that.

Task 5: Gap Check

  • Expressions you could not produce: Which idea was hard to say, such as "a loose plan," "stay flexible," or "became the best part of the day"?
  • Expressions that sounded unnatural: Check whether your verbs, time phrases, and example sentences sounded natural when you read them aloud.
  • Expressions you want to reuse next time: Choose 2-3 phrases from the ideal version that match your own speaking style.

Task 6: Re-Speaking

Speak again for 60-90 seconds after revision.

This time:

  • use at least 2 expressions from your gap check
  • make your example more specific
  • focus on sounding smooth and clear, not perfect

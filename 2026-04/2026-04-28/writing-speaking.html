Writing + Speaking

Topic

Do you prefer arriving early, or arriving just on time?

Think about real situations such as meeting a friend, going to class, visiting a clinic, joining an online meeting, or catching a train.

Task 1: Speaking

Speak in English first for 60-90 seconds.

Try to include:

  • which style you usually prefer
  • why you feel comfortable with that style
  • one real example from your life
  • one good point of that habit
  • one situation where you would do the opposite

Task 2: Japanese Draft

今ほんとうに言いたかったことを、日本語で3-6文くらいで書いてください。

入れてみるとよい内容:

  • ふだんは早めに行くのか、ちょうどに行くのか
  • そうする理由
  • うまくいった、または困った実体験
  • 相手や場面によって変わるかどうか
  • 自分にとって理想の時間感覚

Task 3: English Writing

Use your Japanese draft and write 5-8 sentences in English.

Try to make your writing clear:

  • start with your usual preference
  • add 1-2 reasons
  • give one concrete example
  • mention an exception or contrast
  • end with a short conclusion

Task 4: Ideal Version

Read this after Task 3 and compare it with your version.

I usually prefer to arrive a little early, especially when I have a class, an appointment, or an important meeting. Being early gives me time to relax and check that I am in the right place. It also helps me avoid stress if the train is delayed or something unexpected happens. For example, when I had an online interview, I logged in ten minutes early and used the time to test my microphone and review my notes. However, I do not want to be too early, because waiting for a long time can feel unnecessary. In casual situations, like meeting a close friend at a cafe, I am more flexible. In general, I like having a small time buffer rather than rushing at the last minute.

Task 5: Gap Check

  • Expressions you could not produce: Which ideas were hard to say, such as arrive a little early, avoid stress, review my notes, or a small time buffer?
  • Expressions that sounded unnatural: Check whether your time expressions, reasons, and example sentences sounded natural when you read them aloud.
  • Expressions you want to reuse next time: Choose 2-3 phrases from the ideal version that fit your own speaking style.

Task 6: Re-Speaking

Speak again for 60-90 seconds after revision.

This time:

  • use at least 2 expressions from your gap check
  • make your example more specific
  • focus on sounding smooth and clear, not perfect

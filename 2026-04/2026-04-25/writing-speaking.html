Writing + Speaking

Topic

How do you like to spend a relaxing day off?

Talk about your real habits, your ideal day off, and why that kind of time helps you recharge.

Task 1: Speaking

Speak in English first for 60-90 seconds without writing anything yet.

If you need ideas, talk about these points:

  • Where you usually spend your day off
  • What you like to do first
  • Whether you prefer being alone or with someone
  • What makes the day feel relaxing
  • What kind of day off you do not want

Task 2: Japanese Draft

今度は、日本語で「本当に言いたかったこと」を書いてください。

入れるとよい内容:

  • 理想の休みの過ごし方
  • 実際によくすること、または最近の過ごし方
  • その過ごし方が自分に合っている理由
  • 逆に、休みの日にしたくないこと

Task 3: English Writing

Task 2 で書いた内容をもとに、英語で 6-8 文ほど書いてください。

つなぎ方のヒント:

  • I usually ...
  • One reason is that ...
  • If I have enough time, ...
  • Instead of ..., I ...
  • What matters most is ...

Task 4: Ideal Version

On a relaxing day off, I like to keep my schedule simple. I usually wake up without an alarm and make a slow breakfast at home. After that, I go for a short walk or spend some time in a cafe with a book. I do not want to rush anywhere, because I spend most weekdays following a busy schedule. If I have enough energy, I meet a friend in the afternoon, but I also enjoy being alone. What matters most is having enough quiet time to reset my mind. This kind of day helps me feel more balanced and ready for the next week. For me, a good day off is not about doing something special but about feeling calm and comfortable.

Task 5: Gap Check

  • Expressions you could not produce: keep my schedule simple, have enough quiet time, reset my mind
  • Expressions that sounded unnatural: compare your wording with wake up without an alarm, go for a short walk, I do not want to rush anywhere
  • Expressions you want to reuse next time: What matters most is ..., This kind of day helps me feel ..., not about ... but about ...

Task 6: Re-Speaking

Speak again for 60-90 seconds after revision.

This time:

  • Use at least 3 expressions from Task 4 or Task 5
  • Try to connect your ideas more smoothly
  • Focus on sounding natural, not on speaking longer

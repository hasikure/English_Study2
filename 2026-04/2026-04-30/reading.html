English Text

Schools should offer short, practical programming courses to all students, not only to future engineers. Many teenagers are tired of hearing two extreme messages: either programming will solve every career problem, or it is only for a small group of math experts. Both ideas are misleading. In a world shaped by apps, data, and automated systems, basic programming is a desirable form of literacy. Students do not need to become professionals, but they should understand how digital tools are built and how simple instructions can change a task.

One reason is that programming turns students from passive users into active problem-solvers. A student who learns how to write a short script to rename files, count survey answers, or organize club expenses starts to see technology differently. After one small success, many students are curious to do more. They may want to build a study timer, a vocabulary quiz, or a simple website for a school event. These are modest projects, but they teach logic, patience, and clear communication.

Another reason is that programming challenges the idea that talent is fate. Too many people believe that some children are born for computers and others are not. As a result, a shy literature student may think she is the last person to try writing code. Yet when the lesson is connected to her interests, such as making a poetry website or analyzing word patterns, she often discovers that code is learnable. The first step is rarely brilliance; it is usually repetition.

Finally, the learning environment matters. If teachers want beginners to stay engaged, they must help them make themselves at home. That means using plain language, welcoming mistakes, and giving examples from everyday life. In one after-school class, students wrote a tiny program to divide the cost of ingredients for a cooking club. Nobody changed their fate in one afternoon, but they left with more confidence and a more realistic view of technology.

For these reasons, basic programming should be treated as a common educational tool, not a narrow specialty. The goal is not to create thousands of engineers. The goal is to give more young people the power to ask better questions, test ideas, and understand the systems around them.

最後に、学ぶ環境も重要です。もし教師が初心者の意欲を保ちたいなら、生徒がくつろげるようにしなければなりません。それは、やさしい言葉を使い、間違いを歓迎し、日常生活の例を示すということです。ある放課後の授業では、生徒たちは料理クラブの材料費を分けるための小さなプログラムを書きました。たった半日で誰かの運命が変わったわけではありませんが、生徒たちはより大きな自信と、技術に対するより現実的な見方を持って帰りました。

こうした理由から、基本的なプログラミングは狭い専門分野ではなく、誰にとっても共通の教育的な道具として扱われるべきです。目標は何千人ものエンジニアを生み出すことではありません。より多くの若者が、より良い問いを立て、考えを試し、自分たちの周りの仕組みを理解する力を持てるようにすることが目標なのです。

On Monday morning, I arrived at Haneda Airport earlier than usual for a business trip to Sapporo. Even so, a long line had already formed, and dozens of passengers were starting to queue for the security check. The airport had introduced a new security regime that week, so every bag needed an extra scan. At first, most people stayed calm and tried to carry on as normal. Some opened their laptops, and others quietly drank coffee while they waited.

About twenty minutes later, a problem came to light. One of the scanning machines had stopped working, and the staff had only just noticed it. That was why the line was moving so slowly. A tired airport worker apologized to us and explained the situation through a speaker. He asked everyone to think over their next step and decide whether they still had enough time to catch their flights. A few travelers left the line and went to talk to the airline desk, but most of us decided to stay. In the end, the machine was fixed, the line moved again, and I reached my gate just before boarding began.

Writing + Speaking

Topic

Do you prefer discussing important things by phone, or by text message?

Think about making plans, solving a misunderstanding, checking on someone, or talking with classmates or coworkers.

Task 1: Speaking

Speak in English for 1-2 minutes.

Try to answer these points:

  • Which do you usually choose: phone calls or text messages?
  • Why does that choice work better for you?
  • Describe one real situation.
  • What is one weakness of your choice?

Task 2: Japanese Draft

英語で言いたかったことを、日本語で自然に書いてください。

入れるとよい内容:

  • 自分はどちらを好むか
  • その理由
  • 実際の場面や経験
  • 逆に、もう一方のほうがよい場面

Task 3: English Writing

Write your idea in English in 5-8 sentences.

Try to include:

  • your preference
  • at least one clear reason
  • one specific example
  • one exception or opposite case

Task 4: Ideal Version

I usually prefer sending text messages when I need to discuss something important. Writing gives me a little time to think, so I can choose my words more carefully. This is useful when I need to make plans or explain a small problem clearly. For example, if I have to change a meeting time, I can send all the details in one message, and the other person can check them later. However, if there is a serious misunderstanding, I would rather make a phone call. Hearing the other person's voice makes it easier to understand their feelings and solve the problem quickly.

Task 5: Gap Check

  • Expressions you could not produce: "choose my words more carefully," "check them later," "solve the problem quickly"
  • Expressions that sounded unnatural: "talk important things by message" -> "discuss something important by text message"
  • Expressions you want to reuse next time: "make plans," "explain a small problem clearly," "hear the other person's voice," "serious misunderstanding"

Task 6: Re-Speaking

Speak again for 1-2 minutes after revision.

This time:

  • reuse at least two expressions from Task 5
  • add one specific example
  • use one contrast such as "However," or "On the other hand,"

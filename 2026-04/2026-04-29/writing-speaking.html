Writing + Speaking

Topic

Do you prefer shopping in a physical store, or buying things online?

Think about clothes, books, daily items, gifts, or electronics.

Task 1: Speaking

Speak in English first for 60-90 seconds.

Try to include:

  • which option you usually prefer
  • two reasons for that preference
  • one real example from your life
  • one situation where you would choose the opposite option

Task 2: Japanese Draft

Write what you really wanted to say in Japanese in 3-6 sentences.

You can include:

  • what kinds of things you usually buy
  • why that method is convenient or comfortable for you
  • one memorable shopping experience
  • one case where the other option is better

Task 3: English Writing

Use your Japanese draft and write 5-8 sentences in English.

Try to make your writing clear:

  • start with your main preference
  • add one or two reasons
  • give one concrete example
  • mention an exception or contrast
  • end with a short conclusion

Task 4: Ideal Version

Read this after Task 3 and compare it with your version.

I usually prefer buying things online, especially when I already know what I need. It is easy to compare prices, read reviews, and order from home without spending extra time. For example, when I need books or simple daily items, online shopping is faster and gives me more choices. However, I still like going to a physical store when I buy clothes or shoes, because I want to check the size, color, and comfort in person. I also visit stores when I need something right away and cannot wait for delivery. In general, online shopping is more convenient for me, but physical stores are better when I want to make sure something really fits my needs.

Task 5: Gap Check

  • Expressions you could not produce: Which ideas were hard to say, such as compare prices, read reviews, check the size, in person, or right away?
  • Expressions that sounded unnatural: Check whether your reason sentences and contrast words, such as however and in general, sounded smooth when you read them aloud.
  • Expressions you want to reuse next time: Choose 2-3 phrases from the ideal version that match your own speaking style.

Task 6: Re-Speaking

Speak again for 60-90 seconds after revision.

This time:

  • use at least 2 expressions from your gap check
  • keep one concrete shopping example
  • focus on sounding natural and clear, not perfect
